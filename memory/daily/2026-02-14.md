## Document Quality Standards Established (12:38 GMT)

**Context:** DVLT DD report required 5-agent cleanup + 60 corrections in final audit to reach Big 4 consulting standard.

**Major Lesson:** Professional tone from START, not casual then polish. Emotive language and casual phrasing require extensive cleanup.

**Standards Documented:**

**Created:** `docs/PROFESSIONAL_REPORT_STANDARDS.md` (11.5KB comprehensive guide)

**Updated:** `AGENTS.md` — added document quality standards to startup checklist

**Updated:** `MEMORY.md` — added key standards summary for long-term reference

**Key Standards to ALWAYS Apply in Formal Reports:**

1. **British English:**
   - behaviour, recognised, materialised, analyse, organisation, centre, colour
   - Never behavior, recognized, materialized, analyze, organization, center, color

2. **No Contractions:**
   - has not, does not, cannot, will not, should not
   - Never hasn't, doesn't, can't, won't, shouldn't

3. **Remove Emotive Language:**
   - SHOWSTOPPER → Material risk to viability
   - FATAL → Potentially terminal
   - DESPERATE → Under significant financial pressure
   - CATASTROPHIC → Critical risk to operations
   - BRILLIANT → Well-executed
   - TERRIBLE → Concerning / Inadequate

4. **Professional Terminology:**
   - Patent assertion entity (not "patent troll")
   - Negligible institutional confidence (not "professionals won't touch it")
   - Credibility-building exercise (not "startup buys credibility")
   - Micro-cap equity (not "penny stock")
   - Market manipulation scheme (not "pump-and-dump")

5. **No Casual Phrasing:**
   - Material concern (not "red flag")
   - Circular dependency (not "chicken-and-egg problem")
   - Unsubstantiated claims (not "smoke and mirrors")
   - Materially significant (not "game-changer")
   - High-confidence assessment (not "slam dunk")

6. **Evidence-Based Only:**
   - Every claim needs source OR methodology OR clear label as professional judgement
   - No unsupported assertions
   - Separate facts from assessments

7. **Comparative Statements Need Context:**
   - Never compare without full supporting context
   - If context is deleted, statement must still make sense standalone
   - Orphaned comparisons confuse readers

**60 Corrections Made in DVLT DD Final Audit:**
- 11× emotive language replaced
- 19× casual phrases professionalized
- 17× contractions expanded
- 9× British English corrections
- 1× structural issue (missing numbered item)
- 3× draft artefacts removed

**Quality Rating Achieved:** 9/10 (Big 4 consulting standard)

**Process for Future Reports:**
1. Read `docs/PROFESSIONAL_REPORT_STANDARDS.md` before starting
2. Draft with professional tone from first sentence
3. Multiple review passes (content → structure → tone → technical)
4. Run quality checklist (see standards doc)
5. Final audit pass (use Opus 4.6 for tone consistency if high-stakes)

**Remember:** Professional ≠ robotic. It means credible, evidence-based, respectful of reader's intelligence and time.
